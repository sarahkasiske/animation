Inequalities in Colonoscopy Screenings

There is a vast amount of price inequality for Colon Cancer screenings in cities where poverty rates are high and the median household salary is low. For example, in Detroit the median household’s salary is $26,325 and the average cost of a colonoscopy is $3,208. That is about 12.2% of a households yearly income.

What Should a Colonoscopy Cost?

According to the Healthcare Bluebook the average fair price for a colonoscopy screening is $1,823 dollars in the United States. While this may not be the best price in every situation, looking at sources such as the Healthcare Bluebook might help peopler better understand what a colonoscopy should cost. This understanding could be the ﬁrst step in the standardization of the cost of a colonoscopy.
